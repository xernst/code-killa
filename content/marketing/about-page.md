## Section 1: what this is

Headline: **what is promptdojo**

promptdojo is a free python school for people who are not software engineers
and now have to build anyway. 35 chapters, hundreds of runnable steps, all in
your browser. it's built for the marketer, the analyst, the lawyer, the ops
person whose job just started assuming they can read and direct ai-written code.

---

## Section 2: the thesis

Headline: **why it exists**

ai is reshaping every job, not just the ones in tech headlines. the reskilling
problem of the 2020s isn't "learn to use chatgpt." it's "learn to build with
ai, because your job in three years assumes you can."

most courses teach python like you're trying to become a software engineer.
that's the wrong course for someone who just needs to read what cursor wrote,
know if it's wrong, and fix it. promptdojo is built around that person.

## Section 4: how it works

three moves, every step:

- **read.** a small piece of code or a concept. plain english, no walls of theory.
- **run.** python runs in your browser via pyodide. no install, no venv. the editor is right there.
- **fix.** most steps end with a buggy snippet. you fix it, you run it, you move on. you never write a fizzbuzz.

## Section 6: the model

promptdojo is free for individuals, forever. no paywall, no preview that runs
out. companies pay to train their teams on it, with paths by role and real
completion data, and that corporate revenue is what keeps the individual side
free. see [for teams](/for-teams).

## Section 7: faq

(Rewritten. The old pricing FAQ, the "school lives in the app" line, and the
streak references are gone.)

- **is it really free?**
  yes. all 35 chapters, every runnable step, no signup, no time limit, no
  paywall. free for individuals, forever.

- **how does it stay free?**
  companies pay to train their teams on promptdojo. that corporate revenue
  funds the platform for everyone else.

- **do i need python experience?**
  no. chapter 00 starts before any code. if you've used cursor or claude to
  write something, you already know enough to begin.

- **do i have to log in?**
  no. progress saves to your browser. logging in only syncs across devices.
  no list, no upsell.

- **why isn't it on udemy or coursera?**
  because i wanted it to look how i wanted, run in the browser, and never
  gate-keep behind a streak.
