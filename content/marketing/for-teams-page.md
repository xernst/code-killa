## Section 2: the problem

ai reshaped every role in your company at the same time. your marketers, ops
people, analysts, and support team are already using ai tools to do their jobs.
most of them learned it by guessing.

they can't reliably read the code those tools produce, can't tell when it's
wrong, and can't fix it. that's a quiet risk sitting in every department, and
it grows every week you don't address it.

the usual answer, a one-hour webinar, does nothing. reskilling at this scale
needs a real course and real proof your people finished.

---

## Section 3: what your team gets

promptdojo for teams is the full hands-on course plus the layer a company
actually needs to run it.

- **ten career paths, one per role.** developer, marketer, designer, customer
  service, copywriter, data analyst, project manager, hr specialist, operations,
  lawyer. each team gets the chapters that fit its work, not a generic syllabus.
- **team dashboards.** assign paths by role. see who started, who's moving,
  who's stuck, who finished. real progress data, not a list of logins.
- **completion certificates.** proof a person did the work, not just enrolled.
- **custom quizzes per role.** placement and checks tuned to each path, so a
  mixed-experience team isn't bored or lost.
- **live sessions.** scheduled working sessions that keep a cohort moving and
  answer what a self-paced course can't.

---

## Section 4: how the course works

no slides, no lecture videos. people learn by doing.

every step shows a small piece of ai-written code. the learner reads it,
predicts what it does, finds the bug, and fixes it. it runs in the browser, so
there's nothing for your it team to install. someone finishes a path knowing a
concrete, current skill, not a list of definitions.

---

## Section 5: the model

promptdojo is free for individuals, forever. corporate training is what funds
it. when you train your team, you also keep the platform free for everyone else.
that's the whole model, and it's worth saying to your people: this is reskilling
that does some good on the way.

pricing is per seat, scoped to your team size and rollout. it's a short
conversation, not a quote sheet.
