## who this is for
you practice law or run a legal team. ai already drafts, summarizes, and cites.
you need to build tools around it and, more than anyone, verify what it
produced. a confident wrong answer in this work is not a small thing. this path
gets you there.

## what you'll be able to do
build document-review and research tools that work over your actual documents,
not the model's memory. get structured summaries out of long files. verify ai
output rigorously and catch invented citations, names, and dates. govern how ai
is used across a practice, and handle sensitive data the right way.

## the path
fifteen chapters: the python foundations, files and i/o, calling ai models,
structured output, prompting, context and retrieval, evals, debugging output,
secrets and access, and team skills. about 13 to 15 hours, self-paced.

## how it works
no videos, no fizzbuzz. every step shows a small piece of ai-written code. you
read it, predict what it does, find the bug, fix it, run it. it all runs in
your browser.
