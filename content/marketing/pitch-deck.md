### Slide 2 · the problem

ai is reshaping every job, not just engineering.

marketers, lawyers, analysts, support teams, designers: they're all using
claude and cursor already. almost none of them can read the code those tools
write, catch what it got wrong, or fix it.

that gap is in every department, right now, growing quietly.

---

### Slide 3 · why now

three things happened at once:
- ai coding tools went mainstream outside engineering.
- the job market started pricing in "can build with ai" as a baseline.
- nobody built the course for the people caught in the middle.

the reskilling window is open now. in three years it's table stakes.

---

### Slide 4 · the thesis

the 2020s reskilling problem isn't "learn to use chatgpt."

it's "learn to build with ai, because your job in three years assumes you can."

everyone is becoming a builder, whether they want to or not.

---

### Slide 5 · the product

promptdojo is a hands-on python school for non-engineers.

you read code ai actually wrote. you predict what it does. you find the bug.
you fix it. 833 times, in your browser, no installs.

35 chapters, ten career paths, runs on a free static stack.

---

### Slide 6 · the wedge

every other course teaches python like you want to be a software engineer.
syntax drills, fizzbuzz, build a calculator from scratch.

promptdojo is built backwards. it teaches the python you trip on when you're
reading what ai wrote. read first, fix constantly, skip what ai already does
well.

old courses sell 1995 fundamentals. we sell 2026 fundamentals.

---

### Slide 7 · who it's for

**individuals:** just-graduated, mid-career pivoters, people whose jobs are
already changing. free, forever.

**companies:** teams that need to reskill at scale and need proof it worked.
paid, per seat.

same course. same thesis. two surfaces.

---

### Slide 8 · the model

free for individuals, forever. no paywall, no preview trick.

corporate training funds the platform. companies pay per seat for curated
paths and an accountability layer. that revenue keeps the individual side free.

the free tier is the top of the funnel and the mission at the same time.

---

### Slide 9 · career paths

ten curated routes, one per role: developer, marketer, designer, customer
service, copywriter, data analyst, project manager, hr specialist, operations,
lawyer.

each path is the chapters that role actually needs, and ends with a concrete
exit outcome: a real thing the person can do.

---

### Slide 10 · the corporate layer

what companies pay for, beyond the course:
- team dashboards: who started, who's moving, who finished.
- completion certificates: proof, not enrollment.
- custom quizzes per role and placement.
- live sessions to keep a cohort moving.

the reason training fails is that nobody finishes. this layer is built against
exactly that.
