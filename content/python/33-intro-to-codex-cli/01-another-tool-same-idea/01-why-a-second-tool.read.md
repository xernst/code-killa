# Why bother with a second one

You have a working CLI already. Learning a second one needs a real
reason, so here are the real reasons.

**You stop being trapped.** If you only know one tool, then when that
company raises prices, has an outage, changes something you relied on,
or ships a bad update, you have no move. You wait. A builder who knows
two tools always has a move: switch to the other one and keep working.

**You get a second opinion.** Tools have different strengths and
different blind spots. When one CLI gets stuck on a problem, or gives
you an answer that smells wrong, handing the same problem to the other
one is one of the most useful debugging tricks you'll learn. Two tools
disagreeing tells you something. Two tools agreeing is reassuring.

**You can pick the better fit.** They are not identical. For some
kinds of work one will feel sharper than the other. You can't know
which unless you've actually used both on real tasks.

## This is not a loyalty thing

It's easy to treat tool choice like sports teams. Don't. There is no
prize for being a Claude person or an OpenAI person, and the companies
trade the lead back and forth constantly. The builder who treats these
as interchangeable equipment, picks whichever fits today, and switches
without drama, is in a stronger spot than the one with a favorite.

The cost of the second tool is low, which is the other half of the
argument. Because Codex does the same job in the same shape as the
Claude CLI, learning it is not starting over. It's mostly recognizing
what you already know in slightly different clothes. The next step
lists exactly how much carries over, and it's most of it.
